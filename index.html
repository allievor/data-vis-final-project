    • How to view this visualization

    Stream Graph

    Each colored stream represents a genre. The height of a stream at any given month reflects that genre's total share of all movies released that month. A share is a proportional fraction of a movie assigned evenly across all of its listed genres. For example, a movie with 4 genres would add .25 of a share to each genre it represents.

    Year Filter

    Use the dropdown above to narrow the visualization to a specific year. Selecting a year updates both the stream graph and any open scatter plot.

    Hover

    Hover over any stream to highlight that genre and see a tooltip showing its total share count and the top 3 highest-profiting movies from that genre in the selected time period.

    Click / Enter

    Click a stream (or press Enter when it's focused) to open the scatter plot panel on the right, showing every movie in that genre plotted by production budget vs. worldwide gross.

    Keyboard Navigation

    Tab once to enter the stream graph. Use the Left / Right arrow keys to move between genres. Press Enter or Space to open that genre's scatter plot. Tab again to exit the graph.

    Scatter Plot

    Each dot is a movie. Scroll to zoom in on crowded areas — the bottom-left cluster is low-budget films. Double-click to reset the zoom. Hover a dot for the movie's full financial details. Click ✕ to close the scatter plot.
